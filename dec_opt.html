Decentralized Optimization

There has been much research done on minimizing functions when all information is available to a single agent. In the field of decentralized optimization, we are concerenced with minimizing function when information about the global function is spread out between many agents and communication is costly. There are many questions that arise in this scenario: Under what conditions do the agents agree, and how quickly? Under what conditions do they find the minimizer to the global function? What algorithms work best under what conditions? How do these properties change if each agent operates asynchronously? What happens when communication is sparsified and non-reliable. We studied all these questions and more at my time at NRL.

The Problem

To formalize the problem, in classical centralized optimization, we are often tasked with minimizing a function:

$$ \min_{x \in \mathbb{R}^p} F(x) $$

However, in our case, we have n agents that each have their own local functions $f_i(x)$ and the global function that we are trying to minimize is the collective sum over all these local functions:

$$ F(x) = {1\over n}\sum^n_{i=1}f_i(x)$$

We attempt to find the solution by solving the problem

$$ \min_{x_i \in \mathbb{R}^p} \sum^n_{i=1}f_i(x) $$

Where $N_i$ is the set of all neighbors for

$$ \text{s.t. } x_i = x_j \; \forall \; i,j \in N_i $$ $$ $$ $$ $$ $$ $$ $$ $$ $$ $$
